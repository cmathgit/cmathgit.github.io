পবিত্র

Etymology

Pobitro – Learned borrowing from Sanskrit पवित्र (pavitra) meaning "purifying, averting evil, pure, holy, sacred, sinless, beneficent." From पव (pava, “purification”), from the root पू (pū, “to purify-cleanse”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *pewH-. Bengali: পবিত্র (pobitro) – Pronunciation (Rarh) /pobit̪ɾo/, [ˈpobit̪ːɾoˑ]; (Dhaka) /pobit̪ɾo/, [ˈpobit̪ːɾoˑ]. Comparative: আরও পবিত্র; Superlative: সবচেয়ে পবিত্র. Synonym: পাক (pak), Antonym: অপবিত্র (opbitro).

Macias – Originates from the Iberian Peninsula, a Spanish and Portuguese variant of Matías (Matthew), meaning “gift of God” in Hebrew. Some interpretations connect it to “The Messiah.”

Cruz – Originates from the Latin word "crux," meaning "cross." The name carries religious significance and is strongly associated with the cross, a symbol of Jesus Christ's sacrifice.

Full Name Meaning

When combined, these names mean “Holy Cross of the Messiah” – a name chosen with intentional testimony.

Why This Name Was Chosen

I believe the Holy Spirit led us to choose this name for our son. It is our declaration that God Himself chose it. His name will testify to who Jesus Christ is—to him personally and to all his Bangla-speaking relatives. In Islam, there is no concept of the true Holy Cross. By bearing this name, our son carries a witness that may seem paradoxical to many of our Muslim family members, prompting questions and conversations about the truth of the Gospel.

Choosing an American name might have removed this unique opportunity for witness. My own name, Cruz, spoke to me as an adult—chosen by my mother, it became a silent testimony from her even after her passing. I believe Pobitro’s name will be the same—a voice calling him to the power of the Holy Cross.
