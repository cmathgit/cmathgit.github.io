📋 Teaching Protocol

1. Establish Joint Attention

Position yourself at the infant's eye level. Wait until they are actively looking at your face or hands before showing a gesture.

2. Model & Verbalize Simultaneously

Execute the physical sign while speaking the target word clearly. Say "It is time to eat" while tapping fingertips to lips.

3. Execute in Immediate Context

Sign precisely at the moment the need is being met. Sign milk right before handing over the bottle. Immediate proximity links action to reward.

4. Shape & Reinforce Approximations

Infants produce imperfect versions of signs. Recognize any attempt immediately, verbalize the correct word, model the precise sign again, and fulfill the need.

🎯 Milestones

Receptive understanding (recognition): 2–3 weeks of consistent modeling.

Expressive execution (producing sign): 3–6 weeks after initiation.

Consistency across caregivers is critical — all adults must use the same gestures.
